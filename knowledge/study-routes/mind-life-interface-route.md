## What this route is trying to surface

- where experiential and scientific descriptions genuinely constrain one another
- how informational vocabularies help and where they overreach
- what counts as a careful bridge rather than a premature reduction
- how SUF can scaffold questions about awareness and organization without claiming to settle them

## Companion frameworks or lenses

Phenomenology, philosophy of mind, neuroscience, cognitive science, and consciousness studies should all remain active and not be collapsed into one master vocabulary.

## SUF hook / route note

SUF is most valuable on this route as a cautious bridge framework. It can organize questions about awareness, structure, and relation without pretending that one framework resolves the entire mind-life problem.

## Expected outputs

- a cleaner map of experience-to-model bridges
- stronger discipline around awareness-related crosslinks
- clearer limits on what SUF should and should not claim here
