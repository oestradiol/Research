## What this route is trying to surface

- why high-quality AI work depends on workflow architecture rather than one-shot prompting alone
- how epistemic discipline, interface design, retrieval strategy, and governance fit together
- why graph structure helps some questions while simpler search and retrieval still matter
- how agent roles, constraints, and review gates should be separated
- what a recursive knowledge system needs in order to improve without quietly corrupting itself

## Companion frameworks or lenses

Systems engineering, model-based systems work, HCI, service design, software architecture, knowledge organization, and AI risk-management practice should remain active companions here. This is also the best route for comparing vector retrieval, graph retrieval, and hybrid agentic retrieval patterns without hype.

## SUF hook / route note

This route shows one of the most plausible future uses of SUF outside the current pandemic-governance route: not as a monopoly theory of AI or knowledge, but as a discipline for claim typing, route logic, and boundary control inside a broader design stack led by stronger local methods.

## Expected outputs

- clearer architecture options for AI-assisted knowledge systems
- a reusable agent-role taxonomy for multi-step work
- stronger retrieval and governance heuristics for grounded AI use
- better links between Knowledge, systems design, and future tooling work
