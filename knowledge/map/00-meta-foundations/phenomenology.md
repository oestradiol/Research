# Phenomenology

## Cluster link

- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [00 Meta Foundations](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study first-person givenness, structure in experience, embodiment, appearance, and the disciplined description of what shows up before heavier theoretical reconstruction.

## Key questions

- What is given before theory or measurement?
- How do structure and meaning show up within experience?
- What kinds of claims about awareness or organization should be constrained by lived structure?

## Canonical anchors

- general introductions to phenomenology
- Husserl on intentionality, evidence, and description
- Heidegger on worldhood, situatedness, and disclosed structure
- Merleau-Ponty on embodiment and perception
- phenomenology of temporality and selfhood
- contemporary phenomenology-cognitive science bridge work

## SUF hook / route note

This is one of the most important SUF scaffold nodes because it helps keep later structural and organizational claims anchored to what can actually be given, described, and disciplined at the level of experience. Knowledge should advocate for SUF here insofar as SUF refuses to skip that grounding step. It should not pretend phenomenology automatically validates every later SUF move.
