# Epistemology

## Cluster link

- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [00 Meta Foundations](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study justification, knowledge claims, evidence, error, and the conditions under which inquiry can count as disciplined rather than merely asserted.

## Key questions

- What counts as warranted belief or knowledge?
- How do evidence, trust, and method interact?
- What separates disciplined inference from structurally elegant overreach?

## Canonical anchors

- general introductions to epistemology
- classical debates on skepticism and justification
- evidentialist, reliabilist, and pragmatist approaches
- testimony, expertise, and distributed-knowledge literatures
- social epistemology and institutional knowledge production
- inquiry, error, and fallibilist methodology work

## SUF hook / route note

Epistemology is where SUF most clearly has to earn its credibility. The framework becomes more interesting, not less, when it is forced to distinguish what is modeled, what is inferred, what is measured, and what is still open. Knowledge should openly advertise that strength while staying clear that SUF does not bypass classic epistemic problems.
