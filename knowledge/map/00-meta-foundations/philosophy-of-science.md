# Philosophy of Science

## Cluster link

- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [00 Meta Foundations](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study explanation, models, evidence, theory choice, and scientific discipline, with special attention to how claims should be typed, limited, and compared.

## Key questions

- What distinguishes good models from weak ones?
- How should scientific claims be typed, limited, and compared?
- When does a bridge framework clarify, and when does it quietly inflate its claims?

## Canonical anchors

- general introductions to philosophy of science
- work on models, idealization, and explanation
- realism, anti-realism, and structural realism debates
- confirmation, evidence, and scientific inference literatures
- practice-oriented philosophy of science and methodology debates
- framework comparison and pluralism literatures

## SUF hook / route note

Philosophy of science is one of the best places to explain why SUF is appealing without overstating what it can prove. SUF becomes stronger when its claims stay typed, its bridge moves remain explicit, and its models are treated as disciplined constructions rather than self-authenticating explanations.
