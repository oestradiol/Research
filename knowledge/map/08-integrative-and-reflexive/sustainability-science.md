# Sustainability Science

## Cluster link

- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [08 Integrative and Reflexive](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study long-term human-environment viability, coupled systems, intervention at scale, and the challenge of governing across overlapping ecological, technical, and social time horizons.

## Key questions

- How should ecological, technical, and social systems be studied together?
- What makes an intervention sustainable rather than merely local or short-term?
- How do governance and measurement choices alter long-horizon system outcomes?

## Canonical anchors

- general introductions to sustainability science
- coupled human-natural systems materials
- resilience and long-horizon systems work
- governance and transition literatures
- environmental policy and intervention studies
- interdisciplinary sustainability methodology

## SUF hook / route note

A strong candidate for multi-scale coordination and long-horizon systems analysis. Knowledge should be honest here: SUF helps when sustainability problems are treated as coordination, persistence, and dependency questions across time. It should not be mistaken for the whole of sustainability science, which has its own mature methods and debates.
