- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [06 Social and Institutional Systems](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study power, institutions, governance, state action, collective decision-making, and political order.

## Key questions

- How do institutions structure political action and constraint?
- What explains decision, implementation, and legitimacy across political systems?
- Which political units and comparisons stay bounded enough to remain useful?

## Canonical anchors

- general introductions to political science
- comparative politics and political institutions
- governance and public administration literatures
- state capacity, legitimacy, and implementation studies
- political behavior and collective action work
- crisis governance and emergency politics materials

## SUF hook / route note

Political science is one of the most important places for showing how SUF can travel into governance without losing its discipline. The framework is most promising here when it helps organize questions of coordination, timing, and institutional linkage. It should not be allowed to float above the concrete political and administrative realities that this field studies directly.
