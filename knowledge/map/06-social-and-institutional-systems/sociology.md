# Sociology

## Cluster link

- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [06 Social and Institutional Systems](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study social structure, institutions, interaction, inequality, and collective life at levels where patterned coordination and conflict become visible.

## Key questions

- What kinds of structure shape social order?
- How should agency, institutions, and systems be related?
- Which social regularities are stable enough to support broader coordination claims?

## Canonical anchors

- general introductions to sociology
- classical social theory and institutional analysis
- interactionist and microsociological approaches
- organizational and bureaucratic sociology
- inequality, stratification, and power literatures
- systems and network-oriented sociology

## SUF hook / route note

Sociology is one of the clearest places where SUF can become concretely useful rather than merely abstract. It helps connect patterned organization, coordination strain, and institutional order. Knowledge should still keep this anchored in real social theory rather than treating SUF as a replacement for sociology's own conceptual resources.
