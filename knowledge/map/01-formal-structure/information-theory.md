# Information Theory

## Cluster link

- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [01 Formal Structure](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study encoding, entropy, signal, compression, distinguishability, and the formal handling of uncertainty in communication and system readouts.

## Key questions

- What counts as information in different contexts?
- How do signals, noise, and structure interact?
- When does informational vocabulary clarify a problem, and when does it obscure semantic or organizational differences?

## Canonical anchors

- introductions to classical information theory
- entropy, coding, and communication-channel materials
- signal detection and noise discussions
- links between information and computation
- neuroscience and perception-oriented information discussions
- critiques of overextended informational metaphors

## SUF hook / route note

This is one of the clearest formal bridges into the informational side of SUF. Knowledge should advertise that connection, but carefully: information theory helps most when it sharpens readout, signal, and uncertainty questions. It should not be used as a magical substitute for semantics, experience, or organization-specific explanation.
