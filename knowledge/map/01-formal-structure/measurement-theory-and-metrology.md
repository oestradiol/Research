# Measurement Theory and Metrology

## Cluster link

- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [01 Formal Structure](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study scales, indicators, calibration, validity, and what it means to measure well enough to support comparison and inference.

## Key questions

- What makes a measure meaningful or misleading?
- How should proxies and scales be chosen and checked?
- When does a convenient indicator stop being a defensible readout?

## Canonical anchors

- general introductions to measurement theory
- validity and reliability materials
- calibration and metrology standards
- proxy and indicator design discussions
- scale construction and comparability literatures
- applied examples from science, governance, and public health
