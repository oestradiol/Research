# Dynamical Systems

## Cluster link

- [emerges-from](../../_relations/emerges-from.md) [01 Formal Structure](README.md)

## Study purpose

Study state change, trajectories, attractors, stability, lag, and temporal patterning in evolving systems.

## Key questions

- How do systems change through time?
- What kinds of stability or instability matter for a given domain?
- How do lag, coupling, and path dependence alter what is actually observable?

## Canonical anchors

- introductions to dynamical systems
- nonlinear dynamics and temporal modeling materials
- stability, attractor, and bifurcation discussions
- feedback and coupled-system modeling work
- biological and ecological dynamics examples
- social or governance applications of temporal system models

## SUF hook / route note

This node is central whenever temporal windows, lag, and dynamic persistence are in focus. It is one of the most convincing places to explain why SUF is useful, because SUF is strongest when organization must be tracked through time rather than treated as static structure. Knowledge should still keep this tied to real modeling discipline rather than loose “everything changes” rhetoric.
