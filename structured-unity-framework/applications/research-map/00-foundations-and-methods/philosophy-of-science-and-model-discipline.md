# Philosophy of Science and Model Discipline

## Role in the applications atlas

This node provides the model-discipline layer that keeps SUF from over-reading its own abstractions, proxies, and case narratives.

## Why SUF applies here

SUF needs explicit guardrails about what models do, what they leave out, and how representation differs from explanation or ontology.

## Main objects of study

- representation and invariance
- model choice and underdetermination
- explanatory scope and empirical adequacy
- boundary rules for comparison and inference

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may be models, representational levels, or inferential moves. The recurring problem is category drift: treating a useful model as if it were the same thing as the world it represents.

## Typical scales

- micro: specific model-to-data relations
- meso: families of models or measurement bundles
- macro: program-level architecture and claim discipline

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

This node mostly constrains interpretation. It clarifies how `I`, `C`, `L`, and `U` can be represented, compared, and criticized without inflating them into final ontology.

## One concrete SUF research question

What counts as a responsible comparison between two bounded SUF routes when their unit structures and evidentiary surfaces are not identical?

## One bounded future route

A short methods paper on how SUF uses model families, proxy bundles, and weakening conditions to avoid fake precision.
