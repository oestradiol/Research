# Measurement Theory and Operationalization

## Role in the applications atlas

This node governs how SUF moves from layered concepts to observable indicators without collapsing constructs into whichever metric happens to be easiest to compute.

## Why SUF applies here

The framework's operational layer depends on disciplined proxy families, explicit coding rules, and honesty about what a measure can and cannot stand for.

## Main objects of study

- representational versus pragmatic measurement
- indicator selection and construct validity
- coding rules and scale design
- uncertainty, sensitivity, and robustness

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may be events, measures, scales, or coded relations. The persistent problem is construct leakage: the same operational choice quietly doing the work of multiple concepts at once.

## Typical scales

- micro: event-level coding and variable definition
- meso: metric bundles and family-level summaries
- macro: route-level comparison and aggregation rules

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

This node is central for all four variables. It is especially important for keeping `U` as a dashboard-style conjoint signature rather than a single scalar standing in for the whole framework.

## One concrete SUF research question

Which coding and summary choices preserve meaningful separation between dependency, alignment, lag, and unity-like readouts in the New Zealand route?

## One bounded future route

A methods appendix that compares primary and backup I/C/L bundles under explicit sensitivity tests.
