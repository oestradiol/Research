# Systems Theory and Cybernetics

## Role in the applications atlas

This node provides the vocabulary for regulation, feedback, adaptation, and organized interdependence that SUF uses without reducing the framework to classical cybernetics.

## Why SUF applies here

Many SUF routes involve coupled parts, feedback loops, and adaptation under constraint. Systems and cybernetics provide useful language for those dynamics, but only when kept subordinate to SUF's boundary rules.

## Main objects of study

- feedback and control
- variety and regulation
- system/environment distinction
- adaptive organization and resilience

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units can be subsystems, controllers, channels, or feedback loops. The danger is over-totalization: calling every interacting set of parts a “system” without a defensible boundary.

## Typical scales

- micro: local feedback loops
- meso: coupled subsystems and clusters
- macro: whole bounded systems and environment relations

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

`I` and `C` are most direct here, with `L` entering through feedback delay and control timing. `U` becomes relevant when adaptation persists across the whole bounded system rather than only isolated parts.

## One concrete SUF research question

How should feedback, control, and requisite variety be represented in a bounded institutional response without collapsing legal, political, and public-communication layers into one system diagram?

## One bounded future route

A cross-walk note between Ashby-style regulation language and SUF's I/C/L/U variables for institutional coordination cases.
