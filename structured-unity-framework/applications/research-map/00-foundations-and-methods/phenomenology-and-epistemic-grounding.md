# Phenomenology and Epistemic Grounding

## Role in the applications atlas

This node keeps the atlas tied to first-person givenness, experience, and epistemic modesty rather than letting later formal layers silently become the whole framework.

## Why SUF applies here

SUF begins from an awareness-first discipline. Any research route that forgets that starting point risks confusing operational summaries with the thing they are supposed to illuminate.

## Main objects of study

- structures of experience
- intentionality and perspectival access
- embodiment and world-directed activity
- self/other differentiation and intersubjective access

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units are rarely institutions here. They are experiential episodes, recurrent structures, or analytic distinctions. The main risk is false atomization: treating fluid experiential structures as if they were already discrete operational variables.

## Typical scales

- micro: lived episodes of perception, action, and attention
- meso: recurrent structures of selfhood, temporality, and embodiment
- macro: historically sedimented forms of social experience

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

`I` matters as structured coupling within experience, `C` as persistence of meaningful organization, `L` as temporality rather than institutional lag, and `U` as the upper boundary that later operational readouts must not pretend to replace.

## One concrete SUF research question

What phenomenological constraints should govern any attempt to interpret large-scale coordination metrics as evidence of unity-like organization?

## One bounded future route

A methods note comparing experiential temporality in phenomenology with the explicit `(tau, sigma)` discipline used in SUF's operational layer.
