# Organizational Coordination and Bureaucratic Systems

## Role in the applications atlas

This node widens the lens from state crises to recurring organizational coordination problems inside and across bureaucratic systems.

## Why SUF applies here

SUF's variable set is well suited to organized multi-unit settings where interdependence, sequencing, and delay shape whether a system acts as more than a loose aggregate.

## Main objects of study

- task dependencies and coordination mechanisms
- bureaucratic routines and inter-unit handoffs
- collective attention, reliability, and adaptation
- distributed cognition in organized settings

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units can be teams, divisions, offices, or workflow stages. The main problem is choosing units that preserve meaningful coordination boundaries rather than reproducing org-chart formalities alone.

## Typical scales

- micro: team or workflow coordination
- meso: interdepartmental or interoffice systems
- macro: organization-wide coordinated behavior

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

All four variables matter here, especially in settings where reliable performance depends on heedful interrelating, mutual adjustment, and visible delay structure.

## One concrete SUF research question

What kinds of bureaucratic systems exhibit stable organization-wide unity-like signatures under pressure rather than only local pockets of coordination?

## One bounded future route

A route on emergency operations centres or public-service coordination hubs as organizational systems in their own right.
