# Policy Implementation and Institutional Change

## Role in the applications atlas

This node tracks what happens after formal decisions: translation into practice, ambiguity, conflict, and institutional adaptation.

## Why SUF applies here

Many SUF routes hinge less on announced policy than on whether decisions propagate, stabilize, and adapt across organizations and time.

## Main objects of study

- implementation chains and bottlenecks
- ambiguity and conflict in rollout
- institutional learning and reform after crisis
- adaptation of routines and governance instruments

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may be policy instruments, implementing agencies, projects, or reform episodes. The main issue is deciding whether the right unit is the policy, the organization, or the implementation chain itself.

## Typical scales

- micro: single implementation chains
- meso: linked reform or rollout programs
- macro: long-run institutional change trajectories

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

`L` is often central, but `C` matters just as much because implementation failure often looks like sequencing instability rather than outright inactivity.

## One concrete SUF research question

How can implementation chains be coded so that lag and convergence are visible without confusing symbolic announcements with operational change?

## One bounded future route

A route comparing alert-level announcement timing with observed implementation timing across selected New Zealand units.
