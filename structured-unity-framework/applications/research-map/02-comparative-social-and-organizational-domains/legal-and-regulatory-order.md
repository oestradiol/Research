# Legal and Regulatory Order

## Role in the applications atlas

This node tracks the formal legal powers, regulatory instruments, and oversight structures that constrain and enable bounded coordination systems.

## Why SUF applies here

Coordination routes often fail or change because the legal instrument set, enforcement powers, or review mechanisms shift what can actually be implemented.

## Main objects of study

- statutory powers and emergency orders
- regulatory design and review
- oversight and accountability mechanisms
- compliance and enforcement architectures

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may include statutes, orders, regulators, tribunals, or enforcement bodies. The challenge is that legal instruments and implementing organizations are related but not identical analytical units.

## Typical scales

- micro: specific orders or regulations
- meso: clusters of instruments and oversight mechanisms
- macro: regulatory environments shaping a bounded route

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

This node usually constrains interpretation. It affects `I` through formal dependency pathways, `L` through implementation authorizations, and `C` through the consistency or fragmentation of enforceable rules.

## One concrete SUF research question

How should legal authority be represented in a coordination graph when the same legal instrument affects multiple units differently across time?

## One bounded future route

A route on New Zealand's shift from Civil Defence Emergency Management Act powers to the COVID-19 Public Health Response Act framework.
