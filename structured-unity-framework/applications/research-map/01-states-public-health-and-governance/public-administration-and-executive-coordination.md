# Public Administration and Executive Coordination

## Role in the applications atlas

This node is the main institutional anchor for executive steering, centre-of-government coordination, and cross-agency orchestration.

## Why SUF applies here

SUF is well suited to cases where coordination, sequencing, and implementation pathways are central and where a bounded institutional core can be identified.

## Main objects of study

- centres of government and cabinet support structures
- interdepartmental coordination mechanisms
- executive steering and monitoring
- implementation chains across agencies

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units often include cabinet or prime-ministerial coordination bodies, lead ministries, emergency-management entities, and public-service coordination nodes. The recurring problem is deciding when a formal agency boundary matters less than an operational coordination role.

## Typical scales

- micro: specific interagency decisions or directives
- meso: functional clusters such as strategy, operations, and public alignment
- macro: whole bounded executive-response systems

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

`I` and `L` are usually most visible here, with `C` showing up in directive consistency and sequencing. `U` becomes plausible only if these patterns persist across scales rather than appearing in isolated episodes.

## One concrete SUF research question

How should a centre-of-government coordination apparatus be bounded so that cross-agency dependency is visible without turning the route into a total state map?

## One bounded future route

A comparative note on executive coordination structures in New Zealand and Taiwan during the first pandemic-response cycle.
