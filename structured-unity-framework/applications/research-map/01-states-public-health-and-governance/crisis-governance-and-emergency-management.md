# Crisis Governance and Emergency Management

## Role in the applications atlas

This node focuses on high-pressure coordination under acute disruption, where timing, escalation, and adaptation are central.

## Why SUF applies here

Crisis response makes interdependence and lag unusually visible. It is one of the clearest public settings for studying structured coordination under perturbation.

## Main objects of study

- emergency governance arrangements
- crisis leadership tasks and coordination forums
- escalation and de-escalation decisions
- recovery transitions and after-action learning

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may include emergency-management agencies, crisis cabinets, all-of-government groups, or named response workstreams. The key problem is distinguishing enduring coordination units from temporary task forces that matter only episodically.

## Typical scales

- micro: acute response decisions and escalations
- meso: operational clusters across workstreams
- macro: the bounded response system across the whole event arc

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

All four variables matter here. Crisis cases often make `L` especially visible because decision, communication, and implementation delays have immediate effects.

## One concrete SUF research question

Which crisis-governance structures most clearly change the dependency density and lag profile of a bounded national response system?

## One bounded future route

A route comparing New Zealand's first acute response cycle with a later, lower-acuity emergency-management phase.
