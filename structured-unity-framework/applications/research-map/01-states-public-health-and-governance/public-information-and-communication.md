# Public Information and Communication

## Role in the applications atlas

This node captures official briefings, guidance updates, and public-risk communication as a distinct coordination function rather than an afterthought.

## Why SUF applies here

Public information is where executive decisions, public-health advice, and implementation expectations meet. It is often central to coherence and adaptation.

## Main objects of study

- official briefings and guidance updates
- alert-level communication and framing
- risk communication and community engagement
- feedback between public messaging and compliance environments

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may include lead spokespeople, communications teams, ministries, and campaign structures. The main issue is distinguishing operational communication from broader media ecologies that lie outside the bounded state route.

## Typical scales

- micro: individual briefings or guidance releases
- meso: sustained communication sequences and campaign phases
- macro: the role of communication in the whole response system

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

`C` is often most visible here through consistency and convergence, while `L` appears in the delay between policy shifts and public-facing updates. `U` matters when communication stabilizes system-wide coordinated behavior rather than simply transmitting information.

## One concrete SUF research question

How can public briefings and guidance updates be coded so that communication is treated as a coordination function rather than only a narrative layer?

## One bounded future route

A briefing-sequence route comparing high-acuity and de-escalation communication in the New Zealand case.
