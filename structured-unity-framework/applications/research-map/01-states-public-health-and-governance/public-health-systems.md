# Public Health Systems

## Role in the applications atlas

This node captures surveillance, tracing, testing, guidance, and health-system preparedness as coordinated public-health functions rather than as an undifferentiated health sector.

## Why SUF applies here

Public-health systems combine technical, institutional, and communication pathways that are strongly shaped by timing, implementation, and cross-unit dependencies.

## Main objects of study

- testing, tracing, and surveillance systems
- public-health command structures
- infection-control measures and preparedness plans
- interactions between health-system and all-of-government coordination

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may include ministries of health, public-health units, laboratories, tracing teams, and advisory bodies. The main challenge is preventing the health system from swallowing the entire route when many functions are only partially health-specific.

## Typical scales

- micro: outbreak-control or tracing decisions
- meso: surveillance, testing, tracing, and hospital-preparedness clusters
- macro: the public-health contribution to the wider response system

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

`C` and `L` are often particularly legible through guidance consistency and tracing/testing turnaround. `I` matters where health functions depend on executive, border, or public-information coordination.

## One concrete SUF research question

How does contact-tracing capacity interact with executive and public-information coordination to alter the lag profile of a pandemic-response system?

## One bounded future route

A sub-route on New Zealand contact tracing that links ministry guidance, PHU practice, and public briefing sequences.
