# Infrastructure and Logistics Coordination

## Role in the applications atlas

This node captures supply, transport, warehousing, and service-continuity coordination where material flows make dependency structure unusually visible.

## Why SUF applies here

Infrastructure and logistics systems are high-value candidates for SUF because they involve explicit interdependencies, propagation delays, and service thresholds across organizations.

## Main objects of study

- supply-chain and freight coordination
- essential-service continuity arrangements
- bottlenecks, redundancy, and rerouting
- cross-sector logistics under disruption

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may include ports, distribution centres, freight coordinators, infrastructure operators, or government logistics cells. The challenge is balancing physical nodes with institutional coordinators.

## Typical scales

- micro: individual handoffs and bottlenecks
- meso: sectoral logistics networks
- macro: national or regional continuity systems

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

`I` and `L` are often strongest here, with `C` visible when rerouting and prioritization remain stable under stress. `U` becomes relevant when the system sustains coordinated flow rather than fragmenting into local fixes.

## One concrete SUF research question

What mix of institutional and material units best captures whether a logistics system is acting as a coordinated whole under disruption?

## One bounded future route

A route on pandemic-era freight or medical-supply coordination using an operations-focused boundary rather than a whole-economy frame.
