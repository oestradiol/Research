# Urban and Regional Governance

## Role in the applications atlas

This node tracks subnational coordination where local implementation, regional variation, and public-service delivery interact under a shared policy frame.

## Why SUF applies here

Urban and regional governance cases make scale discipline unavoidable. They are useful for testing how SUF handles nested systems and partial coordination across jurisdictions.

## Main objects of study

- city and regional emergency governance
- metropolitan service coordination
- local implementation of national frameworks
- regional variation in public communication and compliance environments

## Typical units and unitization problems

Units may include councils, regional emergency groups, health districts, or metropolitan service networks. The main problem is avoiding confusion between local administrative boundaries and the operational geography of the route.

## Typical scales

- micro: local implementation episodes
- meso: city-region or regional coordination systems
- macro: national-to-subnational nested coordination

## Likely I / C / L / U relevance

`L` becomes especially important because subnational handoffs often introduce additional delay. `I` and `C` matter when national, regional, and local layers must remain aligned without becoming identical.

## One concrete SUF research question

How does a national coordination signature change when the same route is re-read from a metropolitan or regional implementation scale?

## One bounded future route

A regional emergency-management comparison inside one national response, using a deliberately narrow set of local institutions.
