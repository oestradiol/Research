# New Zealand Monograph Boundary and Corpus

## Purpose

This note is the chapter-scaffolded boundary and corpus-control surface for the New Zealand monograph core.

It does not replace the canonical route-control files. It states what is now stable enough to treat as the current monograph baseline inside the locked windows.

## Current baseline

The New Zealand route is now chapter-scaffolded inside the locked windows:

- the route boundary remains fixed to the core all-of-government pandemic coordination apparatus
- the seven-unit `sigma1` architecture and locked `sigma2` / `sigma3` readings remain unchanged
- the public corpus now has direct DPMC architecture, public-information, and late-transition review surfaces in addition to the existing OAG, Royal Commission, Ministry of Health, Police, and Beehive anchors
- no unresolved high-priority New Zealand candidate remains in the current discovery log inside the locked windows

## Locked controls

- main perturbation interval: `2020-02-28` to `2020-06-08`
- Comparator A: `2019-11-01` to `2020-01-31`
- Comparator B: `2020-05-14` to `2020-06-07`
- source-admission rule: live public URL plus fixed archive URL required for new official additions
- canonical route-control docs: `research-design.md`, `source-corpus-and-coding-protocol.md`, `official-corpus-inventory.md`, and `source-discovery-log.md`

## Current corpus strength

The current monograph baseline rests on:

- oversight chronology and architecture anchors from the Office of the Auditor-General and the Royal Commission
- public-health command and tracing-review anchors from the Ministry of Health, Verrall review, and Allen + Clarke review
- direct DPMC action-plan, operating-system, public-information, welfare, emergency-powers, review, and transition papers
- direct Police implementation and continuity pages
- direct Beehive decision, legal-handoff, and alert-transition pages

That is enough to support:

- a bounded architecture chapter
- a bounded escalation chapter
- a bounded implementation-thickening chapter
- a bounded late-transition and lag chapter
- a bounded robustness and weakening chapter

## Residual non-blocking burden

Later reopening could still add:

- finer DPMC or NCMC operating-model records
- deeper briefing and public-information surfaces
- wider post-window or pre-window extension

Those are now later deepening options, not current high-priority blockers.
