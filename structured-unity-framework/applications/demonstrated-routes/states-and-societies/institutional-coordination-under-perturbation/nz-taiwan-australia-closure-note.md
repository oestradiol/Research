# NZ-Taiwan-Australia Three-Case Closure Note

## Purpose

This file states the bounded pandemic-governance closure across New Zealand, Taiwan, and Australia federal cases.

It documents what coordination patterns travel across the three cases, what remains case-specific, and the explicit limits of cross-case generalization.

## Cross-case empirical base

| Case | Events | Window | Architecture | Key coordination layers |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| New Zealand | 38 | Jan 2020 – Nov 2021 | Unitary state | DPMC executive, All-of-Government, MoH health command, Police implementation |
| Taiwan | 20 | Jan 2020 – Apr 2020 | Centralized health command | CECC cross-agency command, NHCC/EOC architecture, border-health integration |
| Australia | 18 | Feb 2020 – May 2020 | Federal dual-layer | AHPPC health advisory, National Cabinet political coordination, federal-state bridge |

## Patterns that travel across all three cases

### 1. Public-information coordination centrality (strong)

All three cases show `public-information coordination` as structurally central:

- **New Zealand**: DPMC All-of-Government coordination receives public-information dependency in 38/38 events; public-information coordination receives from every sigma1 unit
- **Taiwan**: CECC command center explicitly includes public-information coordination; receives all 20 events
- **Australia**: AHPPC statements and National Cabinet transcripts both route through public-information; receives 18/18 events

**Closure claim**: Public-information coordination is not downstream decoration but structural infrastructure in all three pandemic-governance responses.

**Weakening condition**: If a fourth case shows public-information as consistently downstream-only, revise from "structurally central" to "conditionally central."

### 2. Health-executive advisory bridge (strong)

All three cases show explicit advisory dependency between health command and executive coordination:

- **New Zealand**: MoH → DPMC directive dependencies; Health Act special powers requested by Minister
- **Taiwan**: CECC cross-agency command includes health as core function; NHCC integration
- **Australia**: AHPPC → National Cabinet advisory chain (au-b-010, au-b-005, au-b-001)

**Closure claim**: Health advice to executive is visible and traceable, not black-boxed or retrospective fiction.

**Weakening condition**: If health-executive chains show consistent lag >14 days without justification, weaken "advisory bridge" to "formal consultation window-dressing."

### 3. Border-tightening sequence (moderate-strong)

All three cases show early border coordination distinct from later quarantine implementation:

- **New Zealand**: Feb 2 foreign traveler restrictions → Feb 10 quarantine requirements → Apr 9 managed isolation facilities
- **Taiwan**: Jan 22 airport response center → Feb 8 entry quarantine → Feb 14 system launch → Feb 15 support clarification
- **Australia**: Feb 13 travel restrictions → Mar 15 cruise ban/gathering limits → Apr 24 maritime crew exemption

**Closure claim**: Border tightening follows sequence (restriction announcement → implementation → support/sanction clarification), not single-shot closure.

**Weakening condition**: If a case shows border and quarantine as simultaneous same-day events, weaken to "conditional sequence."

### 4. Implementation-observed vs decision-announced distinction (moderate)

All three cases carry both `decision announced` and `implementation observed` markers:

- **New Zealand**: 21 events implementation-observed; 17 decision-announced
- **Taiwan**: 8 implementation-observed (e.g., tw-p-010, tw-p-017); 12 decision-announced
- **Australia**: 2 implementation-observed; 16 decision-announced (federal scope limits observable implementation)

**Closure claim**: The I/C/L/U schema distinguishes announcement from implementation across cases; implementation chains are partially visible in each.

**Weakening condition**: If implementation-observed events fall below 10% for a case, that case should carry explicit implementation-thinness warning.

## Patterns that remain case-specific

### 1. Emergency management layer visibility (case-specific)

- **New Zealand**: Strong — National Crisis Management Centre, EOC activation visible, DPMC All-of-Government operational
- **Taiwan**: Moderate — NHCC architecture documented but fewer discrete EOC activation events
- **Australia**: Weak — Federal emergency management less visible; state-level EOCs excluded by scope rule

**Limit**: Emergency management layer strength varies by state structure and source availability. Do not claim uniform emergency-management architecture across cases.

### 2. Lag pair cleanliness (case-specific)

- **New Zealand**: Multiple clean lag pairs (Mar 23 alert → Mar 26 business closure → Apr 2 wage subsidy; Apr 9 isolation → Apr 17 provider direction)
- **Taiwan**: One conservative lag pair (Mar 18 quarantine → Mar 29 health-agency routing)
- **Australia**: Lag pairs partially visible but federal-state coordination complicates clean measurement

**Limit**: Lag measurement requires matched event pairs; Australia federal scope adds coordination complexity that limits clean L measurement.

### 3. Federal vs unitary coordination architecture (structural difference)

- **New Zealand**: Unitary — DPMC coordinates through MoH, Police, GoC directly
- **Taiwan**: Centralized — CECC commands across agencies; NHCC unified platform
- **Australia**: Federal — AHPPC advises; National Cabinet coordinates state/territory implementation

**Limit**: Federalism adds explicit state-territory layer that unitary cases black-box. Do not claim coordination architecture is identical across cases.

### 4. Source family composition (case-specific)

- **New Zealand**: OAG audit, Royal Commission, DPMC action plans, MoH circulars, Police reports
- **Taiwan**: CDC press releases, CECC bulletins, NHCC architecture docs, White Paper retrospective
- **Australia**: AHPPC statements, National Cabinet transcripts, PM&C inquiry materials

**Limit**: Source families differ by institutional accountability structures. Comparison must account for source-family heterogeneity.

## Bounded closure statement

**Strong inside pandemic-governance family:**

> SUF shows that public-information coordination is structurally central to pandemic-governance coordination architecture across unitary (New Zealand), centralized-health-command (Taiwan), and federal (Australia) state structures. This is visible in the 76-event empirical base where public-information coordination receives from every issuing unit in all three cases.

**Explicitly bounded:**

- Family: pandemic-governance (viral respiratory disease, 2020-2021 wave)
- Cases: 3 (NZ, Taiwan, Australia federal)
- Architecture: unitary, centralized, federal — not all governance types
- Claim: structural centrality visible, not optimality proven
- Vocabulary: provides I/C/L/U description, not automated prescription

**Weakening conditions (revise if any are met):**

1. Fourth case shows public-information as consistently downstream-only
2. Health-executive advisory chains show >14 day unexplained lag
3. Implementation-observed events fall below 5% in any case
4. Rival framework explains same evidence with fewer assumptions
5. Source audit reveals systematic archive-link decay >20%
