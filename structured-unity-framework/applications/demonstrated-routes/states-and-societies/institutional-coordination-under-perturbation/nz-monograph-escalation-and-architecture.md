# New Zealand Monograph Escalation and Architecture

## Purpose

This note is the chapter-facing escalation and architecture surface for the New Zealand monograph core.

## Stable chapter claim

The New Zealand route should now be read as a coordinated architecture that becomes progressively more explicit across the acute escalation period, not as a loose bundle of headline decisions.

## Core architecture chain

The current baseline architecture chain is:

1. `2020-01-27` national security system activation
2. `2020-03-06` ODESC-directed NCMC activation at the Ministry of Health
3. `2020-03-11` All-of-Government Controller role added
4. `2020-03-19` Covid-19 Ministerial Group replaces the earlier Cabinet committee
5. `2020-04-01` National Action Plan 2.0 and companion DPMC architecture notes show the Quin-centred all-of-government response structure in working form

## What this chapter can now argue

- the strategic centre thickens before the full lockdown sequence, not only after it
- public-information coordination is structurally inside the response architecture, not an after-the-fact wrapper
- the action-plan layer is now directly grounded in DPMC material rather than only retrospective oversight sources

## What this chapter still does not claim

- that the route is exhaustive outside the locked windows
- that architecture visibility alone settles implementation or outcome questions
- that one case generalizes across all domains
