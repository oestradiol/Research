# New Zealand Monograph De-escalation and Lag

## Purpose

This note is the chapter-facing late-transition, review, and lag surface for the New Zealand monograph core.

## Stable chapter claim

The late New Zealand route now shows a formal review-and-transition chain rather than only headline easing announcements.

## Current direct late-transition chain

- `2020-04-14` preparation to review Level 4 status
- `2020-04-20` formal review of Alert Level 4 plus public announcement of the date for Level 3
- `2020-04-22` Level 3 powers and authorisations
- `2020-05-04` preparation to review Level 3 status
- `2020-05-06` preparing for Alert Level 2
- `2020-05-11` formal review of Alert Level 3
- `2020-05-12` national transition-period planning briefing

## Current clean lag pairs

The currently clean public lag surface remains:

- `2020-03-23` Level 3 / Level 4 announcement to `2020-03-25` implementation: `2` days
- `2020-04-20` move-date announcement to `2020-04-28` Level 3 implementation: `8` days
- `2020-05-06` Alert Level 2 preparation to `2020-05-14` Level 2 implementation: `8` days
- `2020-05-11` Level 2 announcement to `2020-05-14` implementation: `3` days
- `2020-05-12` transition-period planning to `2020-05-14` implementation: `2` days

## What changed at the current baseline

The lag values did not need to change for the current monograph baseline. What changed is the direct source basis:

- the Level 4 review is now directly anchored in its DPMC paper and minute
- the April-to-May review chain is less dependent on retrospective reconstruction
- Comparator B now rests on a fuller formal transition sequence
