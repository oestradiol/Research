## Current bounded takeaways

### 1. Both routes already reject a flat "communication is just packaging" reading

Public-information coordination is structurally central in both seeds. The difference is not whether it matters, but how thickly the current official seed makes that role visible.

### 2. Taiwan starter visibility is narrower but sharper around command, border, and quarantine

The Taiwan starter tranche is dominated by CECC-visible command, border control, quarantine support, and compliance guidance. That gives a clean first comparator shape even before a fuller Taiwan ledger exists.

### 3. New Zealand is already thicker on implementation breadth

The New Zealand route currently carries more diverse implementation-side material: welfare support, local-authority and CDEM support, police guidance, checkpoint governance, legal-handoff events, and transition preparation. Taiwan may also contain comparable thickness, but it is not yet assembled in the current archive-clean public seed.

### 4. Command concentration differs in degree, not in kind

Both seeds show strong strategic issuing concentration. Taiwan's starter tranche is more fully command-centre visible because every currently admitted event runs through CECC-facing official material.
