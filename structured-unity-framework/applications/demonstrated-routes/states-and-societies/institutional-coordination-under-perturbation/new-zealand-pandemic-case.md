# New Zealand Pandemic Case

## Parent path

[Institutional Coordination under Perturbation](README.md)

## Why this case is here

This is the first public demonstrated route for applying Structured Unity Framework to a bounded institutional coordination system under perturbation.

## Domain and subdomain

- domain: states and societies
- subdomain: institutional coordination under perturbation
- case: one-state pandemic coordination in New Zealand

## Boundary declaration

The route tracks New Zealand's core all-of-government pandemic coordination apparatus during the first acute national response cycle. It is bounded to the institutional coordination system itself, not to the whole of society and not to a claim about a state's phenomenology.

## Working unit architecture

The working `sigma1` unit set is the seven-unit architecture defined in the Research Program:

1. strategic executive coordination
2. public-health policy and command
3. emergency-management coordination
4. public-service system coordination
5. border-control coordination
6. enforcement and compliance
7. public-information coordination

The working `sigma2` cluster set is:

- strategic coordination
- response operations
- public alignment

`sigma3` is the bounded whole-system reading across the case interval.

## Temporal windows

- main perturbation interval: `2020-02-28` to `2020-06-08`
- Comparator A: `2019-11-01` to `2020-01-31`
- Comparator B: `2020-05-14` to `2020-06-07`

Comparator B is now fixed as the lower-acuity de-escalation window. It aligns with the official alert-level chronology used in the Office of the Auditor-General timeline appendix and the Royal Commission chronology: the route reads the Alert Level 2 period after the 14 May 2020 shift and stops immediately before the move to Level 1 on 8 June 2020.

## Why SUF is relevant here

The case makes dependency structure, coordinated sequencing, lag, and adaptive persistence visible across an institutional system without collapsing the object into a single causal story or an anthropomorphic claim.

## Locked primary readout bundle

- `I`: weighted coordination-dependency graph summaries centered on dependency density, cross-cluster connectivity, and participation breadth
- `C`: rolling alignment-persistence summaries centered on directive consistency, stability of coordinated sequencing, and cross-unit convergence
- `L`: decision-to-implementation lag summaries centered on median lag and dispersion by unit and cross-unit chain
- `U`: a dashboard-style conjoint signature interpreted comparatively across windows and scales, not a universal scalar

## Official grounding surface

The public route is grounded first in official and oversight material:

- [src-oag-covid-response-2022](../../../../references/source-registry.md#src-oag-covid-response-2022) - audit grounding for architecture and coordination boundary.
- [src-royal-commission-phase-one-2024](../../../../references/source-registry.md#src-royal-commission-phase-one-2024) - official retrospective chronology and lessons surface.
- [src-nz-moh-managing-covid](../../../../references/source-registry.md#src-nz-moh-managing-covid) - public-health response and guidance hub.

## Main limits

- not proof of the framework across all domains
- not proof of consciousness for a state
- not a final causal account of outcomes
- not a substitute for later multi-case closure or post-window reopening
