# Taiwan Chapter Boundary and Corpus

## Purpose

This note structures the current Taiwan comparator materials into a chapter-facing bounded route surface for the active `v1.2` pass.

It does not replace the canonical Taiwan route-control files. It states what is already stable, what is still bounded, and what still remains before Taiwan can be treated as fully chapter-scaffolded.

## Current baseline

The Taiwan route now supports an active chapter-readiness pass in bounded form:

- the route boundary remains fixed to official acute command, border, quarantine-support, quarantine-compliance, and public-alignment coordination visible in archive-clean Taiwan CDC / CECC English materials
- the current functionally matched window remains `2020-01-20` to `2020-04-23`
- the current public corpus supports a **`20`-event** bounded ledger (threshold met), not merely a starter-only comparator tranche
- the route now exposes distinct command, border, health, compliance, local-support, transport-routing, and public-alignment surfaces even though a distinct emergency-management layer is still not visible
- the current branch can now be organized as a bounded standalone chapter target, but the active `v1.2` burden is to complete and stabilize that chapter-facing pass rather than to treat it as already closed

## Locked controls

- current comparator window: `2020-01-20` to `2020-04-23`
- source-admission rule: live official URL plus fixed archive URL required for new official additions
- matching rule: functionally matched to the New Zealand route, not date-matched
- functional translation rule: use the New Zealand `sigma1` vocabulary as a translation layer, not as a claim of institutional identity
- canonical route-control docs: `taiwan-comparator-design.md`, `taiwan-official-corpus-inventory.md`, `taiwan-event-ledger-seed.md`, and `first-nz-taiwan-comparison-note.md`

## Current corpus strength

The current chapter-facing baseline rests on:

- CECC activation and cross-agency command-centre language
- first imported-case detection through onboard quarantine and designated-hospital routing
- Taoyuan airport response-centre operating material at the main point of entry
- early home-quarantine tightening for transit and epidemic-area travelers
- foreign-entry restriction and local-district quarantine-routing material
- transit-ban and direct-flight restriction continuity surfaces
- quarantine support, care-centre, health-agency notification, designated transport, and testing-routing surfaces
- cumulative penalties, group-quarantine escalation, and domestic-travel restriction surfaces
- one direct public-alignment surface through phased social-distancing guidance
- quarantine-hotel routing for vulnerable-household cases

That is enough to support:

- a bounded Taiwan boundary and corpus chapter section
- a bounded Taiwan acute coordination chapter section
- a bounded Taiwan implementation and routing chapter section
- an explicit Taiwan lag-limit section
- a bounded NZ-Taiwan comparison section that no longer depends on starter framing

## Current bounded interpretation

The current Taiwan chapter-facing baseline supports four stable readings:

1. Taiwan should not be read as a command-centre-only case.
2. Border, quarantine, support, compliance, and public-information coordination are all visible inside the bounded window.
3. The issuing profile is more split between health and border functions than in the New Zealand route.
4. The lag surface is still thin enough that the chapter must keep an explicit lag-limit section.

## Residual limits

The current chapter-facing baseline still does **not** provide:

- a distinct emergency-management layer
- a second clean lag pair strong enough for serious cross-case `L` comparison
- a later de-escalation chapter comparable to the New Zealand route
- a claim of full NZ-Taiwan symmetry
- a claim that the current Taiwan chapter closes the full Taiwan pandemic route

Those remain active `v1.2` limits, not reasons to reopen boundary architecture.
