# Future Comparative Cases

## Purpose

This note records the bounded comparative sequence for the states-and-societies route.

## Recommended shortlist

### Taiwan

Why it is the recommended first comparator:

- early activation of the Central Epidemic Command Center created a strong coordination surface for timing and command analysis
- border management, quarantine, and contact-tracing capacity offer a useful contrast with New Zealand's island-border strategy
- the official CECC and Taiwan CDC record makes institutional timing visible enough for a bounded comparison

Comparability limits:

- stronger SARS-era preparedness and different geopolitical exposure change the pre-perturbation baseline
- legal, surveillance, and data-governance arrangements differ from New Zealand's public-law environment
- a tighter comparator boundary will be needed to avoid turning the route into a total national-systems comparison

### Australia

Why it is the recommended federal contrast:

- National Cabinet, AHPPC, and Commonwealth-state coordination provide a different institutional topology from New Zealand's more centralized apparatus
- border, quarantine, and public-health responsibilities were more distributed across jurisdictions
- the Commonwealth Government COVID-19 Response Inquiry offers a whole-of-government official record for coordination analysis

Comparability limits:

- federal structure changes the number and type of relevant units
- population scale and state-level heterogeneity complicate direct unit matching
- a bounded Australian route would likely need a narrower Commonwealth-focused or selected-jurisdiction design
