# Terminology Policy

## 1. Why terminology is controlled

High-impact words trigger category drift across phenomenology, science, metaphysics, and interpretation. The public package therefore prefers restrained vocabulary with explicit claim-type discipline.

## 2. Package-preferred wording

- **Unity-like organization** before strong **consciousness** claims at institutional scales
- **modeling stance**, **shared relational frame**, **operational readout**, and **correspondence** for public theory prose
- **multi-agent coordination**, **oversight**, and **socio-technical systems** before anthropomorphic AI language

## 3. High-risk terms and caution rules

**Consciousness, subjectivity, self, spirit, God, universe-consciousness, AGI, child-universe, monism** require explicit scope, claim type, and audience awareness. Broad package prose should prefer lower-risk alternatives.

## 4. AI-language policy

Public SUF text should not try to win credibility by sounding generically polished.

Prefer:

- concrete nouns
- explicit argumentative burden
- bounded claims
- visible source support
- asymmetry and specificity where the material warrants it

Avoid:

- filler signposting
- over-smoothed transitions
- generic assurance language
- detector-style accusations about writing quality

## 5. Detector rule

"Sounds like AI" is not evidence.

When AI-language concerns arise, critique should focus on:

- whether the argument is explicit
- whether the sources are real and appropriate
- whether the claims outrun the support
- whether the prose hides uncertainty behind polish

The package treats AI-detection systems as unreliable enough that they should not be used as high-stakes adjudicators in public argument. See `../references/source-registry.md#src-acm-generative-ai-detection-2023` and `../references/source-registry.md#src-cheng-ai-detection-tools-2025`.

## 6. Anti-anthropomorphism note

AI systems, multi-agent workflows, and institutions may display coordination structure without thereby displaying lived subjectivity. Public wording should keep that distinction visible. See `../references/source-registry.md#src-bender-koller-climbing-nlu-2020`.

## 7. Naming notes

This package uses **Research Program**, **Literature Guide**, **Source Registry**, and **Citation and Archival Policy** as its standard public-facing names.
