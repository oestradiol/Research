## Monograph chapter scaffold

### Part I — Framework and methods

1. **Introduction**
   - Research question: How do states coordinate under perturbation?
   - Target concept: Unity-like organization
   - Contribution: Structural vocabulary (I/C/L/U, sigma1 units)

2. **Framework foundations**
   - Perturbation as coordination probe
   - Dependency, not just decision
   - I/C/L/U vocabulary
   - Scale logic (sigma1-sigma4)

3. **Methods**
   - Event ledger construction
   - Source admission rules (live + fixed archive)
   - Unit architecture mapping
   - Lag measurement protocol
   - Null and weakening conditions

### Part II — New Zealand monograph core

4. **New Zealand case design** — window boundary (Jan 2020 – Nov 2021), unit architecture (DPMC, MoH, Police, GoC), source families (OAG, Royal Commission, DPMC, MoH, Police)
5. **Activation and escalation** — first case detection, alert level system activation, border closure (Mar 2020)
6. **Implementation thickening** — quarantine establishment, contact tracing scaling, managed isolation facilities (Apr 2020)
7. **Late transition** — DPMC authority routing, reopening decisions, action plan architecture (Nov 2021)
8. **De-escalation** — alert level reduction, transition to management phase, oversight and review layer

### Part III — Comparator chapters

9. **Taiwan chapter** — 20-event acute window; CECC activation + NHCC architecture; border-health integration; one conservative lag pair; bounded limits
10. **Australia federal chapter** — 18-event acute window; AHPPC health advisory layer; National Cabinet political coordination; federal-state bridge visibility; bounded limits

### Part IV — Comparative closure

11. **Three-case synthesis** — 76 events across NZ/Taiwan/Australia; patterns that travel (public-information centrality, health-executive bridge, border sequence); patterns that remain case-specific (emergency management layer, lag cleanliness, federal vs unitary); explicit non-closure claims
12. **Rival-framework positioning** — executive-command-only accounts; communication-as-downstream accounts; loose crisis-governance accounts; where SUF adds analytical value; where SUF remains bounded
13. **Weakening conditions** — boundary changes; coding fragility; comparator pressure; route-specificity; alternative-framework victory

- C. Australia event ledger (18 events)

## Bounded closure note

### Target question

What bounded family-level closure could the package reasonably support without silently inflating one demonstrated route into universal proof?

### Current status

Closure is still future work. The package is stronger at demonstrated-route architecture, bounded comparator logic, and monograph scaffolding than at finished family-level closure.

### Required case set before promotion

- New Zealand monograph-baseline route
- bounded Taiwan comparator
- at least one further tightly bounded comparator before stronger closure claims

### Target closure tests

- does the locked payoff survive comparator pressure?
- does the windowed reading remain useful under comparison?
- do route-local measurement choices remain bounded rather than silently universalized?

### Weakening conditions

- comparator failure
- route-local structured evidence organizer breakdown
- evidence that public-information coordination is peripheral rather than central in the comparator family

### Non-claims

- no claim that current package already yields finished family-level closure
- no claim that one route settles all rival framings

## Current bottleneck order

1. keep New Zealand stable and bounded
2. keep Taiwan bounded and honest
3. avoid front-door inflation
4. only then reopen broader closure work
