# Framework Overview

## 1. Executive overview

Structured Unity Framework presents a layered way to think about unity-like organization: when a structured system behaves as more than a loose aggregate at a given temporal window and structural scale. The stack separates phenomenological grounding, modeling vocabulary, dynamic variables, and explicit bridge rules so phenomenology, science, and interpretation do not collapse into one doctrine.

## 2. Three substantive layers plus one bridge/control layer

1. **Structural Phenomenology** - awareness-first epistemic ground
2. **Informational Awareness Framework** - bounded structural modeling and bridge vocabulary, not a finalized ontology or finished technical theory
3. **Unity Dynamics Framework** - `I`, `C`, `L`, and `U` at explicit `(tau, sigma)`; the most technical-facing layer, but still not a closed predictive theory
4. **Framework Interface** - bridge/control layer for claim types, weak naturalization, non-circularity, and scope discipline

## 3. Why the stack is split

- different layers answer different questions
- merging them invites category drift and overclaim
- methods and literature sit beside the theory core rather than replacing it
- the bridge/control layer exists to keep the other three from being silently collapsed into one voice
